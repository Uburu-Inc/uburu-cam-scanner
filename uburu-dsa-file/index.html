DATA SHARING AGREEMENT FOR PROVIDER

This Data Sharing Agreement (the “Agreement”) is between Provider (“PROVIDER”) and Verified Users (the “Recipient”) through app.uburu.ai (the “Platform”) created by Uburu Technology Platforms Limited ("Uburu").

PROVIDER, the Verified Users/Recipient, and Uburu are hereinafter jointly referred to as the “Parties” and each individually as a “Party”.

INTRODUCTION

WHEREAS, Uburu has created a data capture technology platform (app.uburu.ai) enabling health data sharing for patient record exchange and clinical trial recruitment;

WHEREAS, PROVIDER and Recipient desire to collaborate and leverage the platform (app.uburu.ai) created by Uburu for patient-authorized record exchange and/or clinical trial recruitment (the “Project”);

WHEREAS, in performing activities of this collaboration in line with the purpose of the Project, PROVIDER “may” release to, or receive from Recipient under a patient-initiated record exchange, confidential medical records belonging to them as requested from time to time;

WHEREAS, the Parties wish to enter into this Data Sharing Partnership Agreement to clearly state and address obligations arising from the provision of Health Data; for the following identified purpose(s) further to the Project:

  • To enable the receipt or release of patient-authorized medical record;
  • To enable the identification and engagement of doctors with eligible patients to benefit from clinical trials;
  • To enable the use of anonymized or tokenized medical images (radiology and pathology) for AI/ML R&D.

THEREFORE, in consideration of the foregoing, the parties agree as follows:

1. DEFINITIONS

The following terms are defined for this Agreement. Terms used, but not otherwise defined in this Agreement shall have the same meaning as those terms in the Privacy Rule.

a) NHA means the National Health Act 2014.

b) AI/ML means Artificial Intelligence/Machine Learning.

c) Project Participants means Researchers, Pharmaceutical Companies, Healthcare Stakeholders, Development Partners, and PROVIDERs.

d)Health Data: means information relating to the physical or mental health of an individual, including the provision of health care services, which reveal information about the individual’s health status.

e) Individual: is the person who is the subject of protected health information.

f) Personal Health Information or PHI: means information, maintained or transmitted in any form or medium, that: (i) relates to the past, present, or future physical or mental health or condition of an individual; the provision of health care to an individual; or the past, present, or future payment for the provision of health care to an individual, and (ii) identifies the individual or to which there is a reasonable basis to believe the information can be used to identify the individual.

g) Recipient: means

  • For record exchange purposes, a Patient.
  • For clinical trial purposes, a registered principal investigator, clinical trial site or research institute.
  • For AI/ML R&D, a registered researcher or company.

h) Provider: means a health data controller e.g., Hospital, Lab, Pharmacy, HMO, Radiology center etc.

i) Required by Law: means a mandate contained in the law that compels an entity to make a use or disclosure of protected health information and that is enforceable in a court of law.

j) Project Request: means

  • a request made by Recipients on app.uburu.ai for their health record.
  • a request made by Recipients for research purposes.

k) request made by Recipients to PROVIDERs on app.uburu.ai that comprises the data needed and other necessary documents aiding ethics approval.

l) Ethics Approval Committee: means the authority within PROVIDER’s health facility that accepts or declines requests for the provision of data.

m) API: Application Programming Interface

n) Uburu Wallet: is a wallet system available to verified PROVIDERs and Recipients for the purpose of funds transfer after service and into other accounts.

o) Quality Assurance Phase: means a period of internal processing of PROVIDER’s data, undertaken by Uburu.

p) Effective Date: means the date PROVIDER through its authorized representative executes this Agreement by electronically signing its name at the bottom of the form.

2. PROVIDER AS A PROVIDER

PROVIDER must meet the following requirements:

a) PROVIDER must be licensed to provide health-related services in its jurisdiction of practice (evidenced by license certificates and subject to verification by Uburu.

b) To provide dedicated staff needed for the upload or download of paper/manual patient records where PROVIDER does not have or use a local or cloud database.

3. OBLIGATIONS OF THE RECIPIENT

a) To not use or disclose PROVIDER’s Health Data in any manner other than as stated in each project request and as permitted by PROVIDER’s ethics approval committee, this Agreement or as required by applicable

b) To accord special treatment and protection to Information shared by PROVIDER;

c) To use appropriate administrative, technical, and physical safeguards to prevent any use or disclosure of PROVIDER’s Health Data not authorized under this Agreement.

d) To pay all the fees on each Project invoice as stated in the Schedule of Fees here: uburu.ai scheduleoffees. scheduleoffees The fees shall be reviewed by Uburu from time to time subject to market conditions (with at least 24 hours’ notice).

e) To adhere to the standard of privacy contained in the privacy notice available on Uburu's website here: uburu.ai/privacy, and the Terms of Use here: uburu.ai/termsofuse

4. OBLIGATIONS OF PROVIDER

a) Ensure that PROVIDER procures all authority required by its internal policies and under relevant regulation needed to perform all its obligations under this Agreement;

b) Where PROVIDER does not have/use a local or cloud database or voluntarily declines access to their local or cloud database, PROVIDER shall only be eligible to participate in patient record exchange projects and agrees to provide dedicated staff needed for the upload or download of paper/manual patient records within 24 hours of a request. Where PROVIDER cannot provide qualified staff for this purpose, Uburu shall provide such support further to agreed terms;

c) Where a PROVIDER uses a local or cloud database and consents access, PROVIDER agrees:

i. To provide their APIs to connect their database to Uburu’s data infrastructure platform;

ii. Where a third-party IT Vendor manages PROVIDER’s database, PROVIDER will speak with their IT Vendors who shall provide API access to connect PROVIDER’s database to the uburu.ai platform;

iii. Third-party IT Vendors shall be compensated in line with the schedule of fees here: uburu.ai schedule of fees;

iv. To promptly inform Uburu within 5 (five) days whenever its third-party IT vendor is changed;

v. That API connection with uburu.ai legitimizes uburu.ai's extraction of data from their database to meet Recipients requests in projects;

vi. That advanced data processing methods such as tokenization and encryption may be utilized to support cybersecurity and precision in patient data retrieval and clinical trial recruitment projects;

d) Ensure measures to prevent any undue interference, manipulation, coercion of PROVIDER’s staff during data upload or download in furtherance of the Project;

e) Ensure that the information uploaded is correct, complete, and up-to-date as required for the purpose for which it is being shared. Without this, any decision made on the information may be flawed, and inappropriate actions may result. If PROVIDER is aware of a material inaccuracy or omission in the information, PROVIDER must inform Uburu of that inaccuracy or omission;

f) PROVIDER undertakes to indemnify Uburu against legal liability for a negligent act, accidental error, or accidental omission which may be incurred in circumstances where the subject of the exchange of information suffers loss as a result of the inaccuracy of the information and brings an action claim or demand as a Provided that this indemnity shall not apply where PROVIDER establishes that the error did not result from any willful wrongdoing or negligence on its part.

g) PROVIDER agrees that connection with uburu.ai legitimizes uburu.ai's extraction of data from their database/dashboard to meet Recipients requests in projects;

h) To use appropriate administrative, technical, and physical safeguards to prevent any use or disclosure not authorized under this Agreement of Recipient’s health data shared to PROVIDER.

i) Accept that by executing this Agreement it is adhering to the standard of privacy contained in the privacy notice, and the Terms of Use available on Uburu's website here: https://uburu.ai/legal

5. UBURU’S ROLE

In connecting PROVIDERs to Recipients through the platform, Uburu shall play the following role:

a) Constantly supervise all collation of data from and to PROVIDER on behalf of Recipients to ensure proper procedure required by law and in line with this Agreement.

b) Ensure that an obligation of confidentiality is imposed on persons authorized to handle PROVIDER’s Health Data and take reasonable steps to ensure the reliability and integrity of any of its personnel who have access to the Information.

c) Report to PROVIDER, through its Data Protection Officer (DPO), any use or disclosure of PROVIDER’s Health Data not authorized by this Agreement that Uburu or its agents become aware of within ten (10) days of discovery.

d) Make available, at PROVIDER’s request, any internal practices, books, and recordings, including policies and procedures, relating to the use, disclosure, and security of the Health Data for purposes of determining Uburu’s compliance with this Agreement and to the PROVIDER privacy standards.

6. CLINICAL TRIAL and AI/ML RESEARCH

a) Besides participating in patient record exchange projects, PROVIDER having a local/cloud database is eligible to participate in clinical trial and AI/ML research projects

b) For Academic medical centers, the relationship under this Agreement will be exclusive to Uburu.

7. USES OF DATA

a) The Recipient shall use PROVIDER’s Health Data for purposes stated in the project requests.

b) The Recipient shall not use the Health Data shared by PROVIDERs for purposes outside those specified in the project requests or this Agreement.

8. DATA RETENTION AND ARCHIVING

Uburu shall preserve for sixty days (60) the Health Data shared by or to PROVIDERs in a format that makes it easy for Recipients to retrieve after the consent of PROVIDER. Uburu shall ensure that appropriate technical and organizational safeguards are in place such as data minimisation, pseudonymisation, and encryption. Uburu shall delete all Recipient’s Health Data after the sixty days (60) retention period at which time, Recipients can no longer retrieve said Health Data from the Uburu platform.

9. COMPLIANCE WITH PRIVACY RULES

a) Each Party agrees to handle any Personal Information or sensitive information (as defined in the Nigeria Data Protection Act) collected, disclosed, transferred, received or otherwise used by/to/from the Party under all applicable privacy and data protection laws.

b) The Parties shall at all times adhere to Uburu’s Privacy Policy and Terms of Use which are deemed to be incorporated into this Agreement and can be accessed at https://uburu.ai/legal

10. TERM AND TERMINATION

a) Term. The Term of this Agreement shall commence as of the Effective Date and would be renewable annually unless terminated, upon which all of PROVIDER’s Health Data is destroyed and certified as destroyed, in writing, to PROVIDER through its Privacy Officer or any such officer charged with the duty of protecting PROVIDER’s PHI.

b) Termination. If PROVIDER becomes aware of any use of PROVIDER’s Health Data that is not authorized under this Agreement or required by applicable law, PROVIDER may (i) terminate this Agreement upon notice, (ii) disqualify (in whole or in part) the Recipient or Recipient’s authorized agents from receiving PROVIDER’s Health Data in the future, and (iii) report the inappropriate use or disclosure to Uburu. If Uburu cannot resolve the breach within three weeks, report the violation to the Nigeria Data Protection Commission, as appropriate.

c) Effect of Termination. The Recipient will destroy all of PROVIDER’s Health Data and provide written certification to PROVIDER through its Privacy Officer that it was destroyed, including all of PROVIDER’s Health Data that is in the possession of Recipient’s agents. No copies of PROVIDER’s Health Data may be retained.
